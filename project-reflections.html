Walmart vs. Amazon Project Reflection

The purpose of the Walmart vs. Amazon project was to predict who would dominate the retail face by 2040, Walmart, Amazon, or an unexpected 3rd party. My role was to research innovation but I feel like we all took on many roles. Like, Sarah was the one who did the slide designs and I felt like I took charge but I should’ve stepped back a bit, and even though I feel like what I said or did wasn’t wrong, there were much better ways of handling situations. We were mainly hindered by the communication aspect, for example, we did not accurately communicate how much time each person should spend on their slides during the presentation. For example, Sarah and I made a SWOT analysis the day before presenting without communicating this to Rithvik or Charlie. I applied what I learned from the 10 Skills in 10 Days, mainly from the slide designs of the people presenting along with how to speak clearly and accurately. I used credible data, which I learned to identify from my AP Seminar class, which is a class that purely focuses on research. I think we had a good analysis based on our time. We could have improved the slide designs a bit but none of us spoke up except for Sarah because she mainly had that creative touch. I learned that I need to sometimes step back and let people solve things for themselves rather than telling them what to do not only because they have to learn but because they will feel better about themselves. This will help me succeed because I understand how to work with people who care about projects, in contrast to school, where people tend to make me do everything only for the grade. This is something I will not only apply for future projects but something that I wish to apply in my entire life.

Grand Savings Bank Project Reflection

In this project, my team and I had to figure out a way to get Grand Savings Bank from an 18% Gen-Z rate to 25%. We all worked on the ideation but after that, my main job was financial analysis. The skills I had to apply were my mathematical skills along with my skills in Excel. We decided to create an extremely good kids' checking account as our main product, as long as a student account was predicted to bring a lot less revenue but with easier implementation. The kid's account, on the other hand, was planned to increase the parent's interest rate, have an interest rate higher than the competition, and offer a free personal finance class while mainly being pushed by the employees to the parents of existing members. The main hardship within the team was which benefits would be worth the cost of being implemented by bringing more customers which would overall lose us money. The personal struggle I had with this project was by the time we figured out the features we wanted in the accounts, I did not have much time to do the financial analysis, and my teammates, no disrespect to them were great to work with, were unable to help me on the financial analysis due to it being higher level math with the percentages, the prediction, and the ability to apply math in a real-world situation.
