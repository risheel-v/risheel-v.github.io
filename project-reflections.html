My role was to research innovation but I feel like we all took on many roles. Grand Savings Bank Project Reflection

In this project, my team and I had to figure out a way to get Grand Savings Bank from an 18% Gen-Z rate to 25%. We all worked on the ideation but after that, my main job was financial analysis. The skills I had to apply were my mathematical skills along with my skills in Excel. We decided to create an extremely good kids' checking account as our main product, as long as a student account was predicted to bring a lot less revenue but with easier implementation. The kid's account, on the other hand, was planned to increase the parent's interest rate, have an interest rate higher than the competition, and offer a free personal finance class while mainly being pushed by the employees to the parents of existing members. The main hardship within the team was which benefits would be worth the cost of being implemented by bringing more customers which would overall lose us money. The personal struggle I had with this project was by the time we figured out the features we wanted in the accounts, I did not have much time to do the financial analysis, and my teammates, no disrespect to them were great to work with, were unable to help me on the financial analysis due to it being higher level math with the percentages, the prediction, and the ability to apply math in a real-world situation.

We had to apply our creativity and decision-making skills as a team during this project. Still, I had to mainly use my mathematical skills, which I enjoyed because I love math. It felt very fulfilling to apply what I've been learning for so long, do what I enjoy, and apply it to a real business situation and then see the result unfold. I am also really interested in research, having taken a research class in the past with 3 unpublished research papers, which I was able to apply to this project, knowing what to look for in my information, like getting it from the Bureau of Labor and Statistics and the United States Census. While we could have further analyzed our predictions for the social media campaign, it was not our main revenue maker, but we still wanted to have a baseline of what we may bring in, so after finding our information, we decided to underestimate it.

While we did not win the project, it was very fun to work on and my team was great to work with. I learned from this to be more open-minded, like when we should have increased the parent's interest rate, I assumed that would be a lot of money even though it was a small percentage, but after doing my research and running the numbers, I realized it was not as much as I thought, which taught me I need to do my research on all ideas because I am still not a profession with a deep understanding of how everything works. After doing this project, I learned that I enjoy finances and while I never thought of it as a career option before, I may consider it now.

Grand Savings Bank Project Reflection

During this project, we were given a set of data on an Excel sheet that had information on the issues reported to Toshiba by clients and during the developmental phase, but we focused on only the ones reported by the clients. Each issue included a variety of descriptions, but we focused on the date submitted, who reported it, who it was directed to, the issue's age, its developmental stage, and the product. We had to use this information to make graphs and charts on a series of questions that they asked, for example, they asked us to make a graph sorted by date with only open/working issues reported to the Base Software Development team using only issues reported by the client. In addition, we had to come up with our key performance indicator on what Toshiba may need to look into to have fewer issues overall. We used PivotTables in Excel, which was difficult because none of us had ever done this, and because of this, we all had a very similar role in the beginning.

When we first started to sort the data, I tried sorting it using Excel shortcuts, like the "sort" button, then used the automatic graph creator to make the graph. This took a lot longer and was less reliable than using a PivotTable, which Will first learned how to use then taught us all. Once we knew how to sort the data, the main issue was analyzing it. I have a decent tech background, so I was able to understand what the data meant and also teach my teammates what it meant. Raina and Daniel was focused on how we could put it into a presentation, where they came up with an idea to give a potential cause and a potential solution to each graph. While we were all new to this and not experts, our collective teamwork and dedication helped us understand this and contributed to our success.

I learned so much from this project, especially about Excel. When we learned how to use PivotTables, we were able to accurately and conveniently filter the data rather than organizing it by hand. Additionally, when there was a problem, we were able to fix it quickly rather than having to redo the entire data. This has taught me a lot about data analytics and how I feel that this is important because, in a big company, not everyone knows everything. For example, if someone has only been in the company for a year, but wanted to solve a problem from 3 years back, someone who has been working there for a while may already know the story behind the issue, but the newer person can use the data to understand the story and what happened on a deeper level than can be expressed simply with words and observations.
