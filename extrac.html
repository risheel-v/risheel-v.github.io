RESEARCH PAPER DRAFTS

AI's Effects on the Job Market

In 2023, I started a research paper on the effects that the development of AI may have on the job market over the next decade. I used scholarly sources to find quantitative data to make predictions on what may happen in the future to make predictions along with finding expert testimony to back up these findings. From this, I found that while AI may take some jobs, it is more likely to take the jobs most people not only don't want but also that are not being filled. While there are some jobs that AI is going to take which people want, it will create a lot more jobs than it takes. Beyond this, when interviewing higher-ups at Walmart, they said that they are more focused on sparking innovation, meaning they are likely to move those people to a different field. When talking to another AI specialist, he said while AI can take jobs if we want to remove the bottom line, most companies make money from innovation, so it is more likely that those people will be moved to different jobs. This means that it is likely AI will cause some disruption, but the evidence points to the fact that more jobs will be created than taken by AI, meaning that we will have to consider new ways to educate students about new and obsolete career opportunities in the future.

Effects of Increasing the Minimum Wage

In 2024, I started a research paper on the effects of increasing the federal minimum wage in the United States on the cost of living but also how it would affect those in lower-class households. I used the same skills that I learned from my first paper and found that increasing the minimum wage would severely increase prices, as it has historically, but since companies have higher costs, they will likely cause unemployment. Since people of lower-class households tend to have less stable forms for attendance and travel, for example, their car not working or other problems that could be solved by having higher quality things, they are likely to be the first ones to be unemployed. That paired with the increased prices may overall make the living conditions of the lower class worse, while making those who still keep their jobs and are just above the poverty line have worse lives too. My recommended solution to decrease the effects of this was to have federal programs to help those who are struggling. Rather than increasing taxes, we could disband obsolete government programs. I found a source that showed government programs back in the late 1900s that were obsolete already along with how much funding they take; we could use the funding from these programs and reallocate them to help those in need.
