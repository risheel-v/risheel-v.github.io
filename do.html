10 Skills Reflections

During the 10 Skills in 10 Days activity, I tried to pick up how the speakers succeeded and how they got where they are. From Noah Washington, I learned not to be afraid of losing money and that in the early stages it is bound to happen along with the fact that not everything has to be set in stone and it’s ok to be fluid. Cat Hutchings taught me that even if you are not the one executing everything, you can still have a good business by being the visionary and having an implementer. Lisa Chiles talks about how it’s important to dress for occasions, not just always being professional by knowing the audience and what they’re wearing too. Ethan Martinez showed me the importance of unexpected friendships and how they can professionally help you too. During the teamwork day I learned how to effectively communicate to others without words along with concise methodologies. From Laura Easley I learned that one bad decision isn’t the end of the world and that even if something bad happens, you wouldn’t be who you are without it, making me less self-conscious when I make a bad decision. She also taught me about different personality types having different ways of solving tasks and different expectations. Ms. Wieser taught me how it is important to have different kinds of people on a team to ensure you have different perspectives and points of view. Edwin Ortiz taught me that it is important to take advantage of new opportunities that arise and to see how you can capitalize on changes in life. He also taught us that a problem has many solutions that you can do to solve it. Elena Haskins taught me about reverse engineering, which is a very good way to think about a problem and make solutions. My favorite one of them all, Brian Clark, taught me about a lot of things, but something that really stuck with me was when he was talking about listening and said something along the lines of, “when you listen, don’t think of what you’re going to say next while they are talking,” because that interferes with how well you listen to them. Overall, the 10 Skills was very helpful and I am glad that we had the opportunity to hear from these amazing business professionals.
